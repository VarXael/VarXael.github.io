Alien

This project was done to hone my skills with blueprints as well as build a demo game to refine and test my abilities as a game developer.

Total Development time:
2 Months.
Current Status:
Project currently on pause.
Role:
Game Creator.
Tools:
Unreal Engine, Blueprint System.

Details

Listed below are some video references of the game while i was developing. They are put in chronological order of development.

For starters, i created a moving system very similar to the alien from the game Alien vs Predator (2001). I decided to make the movement more "powerful". This was achieved in two ways: First of all, the alien can double jump. The second jump does not launch the alien upwards, but instead it launches the alien forward where the camera is facing. Second of all, i gave the alien a slingshot (represented by the yellow ball in the video. This yellow ball represents a tentacle coming out of the alien and attaching itself to surfaces. When the tentacles attaches to a surface, if the player lets go of the tentacle button, the alien will jump in the direction where the camera is facing. This jump is stronger than the simple double jump.

After the movement i moved on the main mechanic of the game: Possession! I had this idea in mind when starting this project, where it could be really cool if the alien could possess an enemy and fight with its body. In the video, the floating spheres represent the head of an enemy. When throwing the tentacle towards one of these spheres, the tentacle will attach to it. By releasing the button of the tentacle, the alien will launch itself towards the floating sphere and will possess it! Possessing an enemy simply lets you control their body and use any weaponry they may have, as well as any particular movement abilities they may be possess. While in an enemy body, you can still use your tentacle. If you throw your tentacle on a surface and release the tentacle button when it has attached to such surface, the alien will come out of the body flying towards where the camera was facing!

Successively, I moved onto creating some features which i assumed would work well with an alien. I tested out different things until i set myself on creating a sonar-like mechanic that can detect foes and useful items around the level. In the video we can see the final result.

Finally, i decided to further polish up the mechanics I've created and assemble a very small level where all the mechanics can be seen in action. In the video we can see the final result!

Additional Notes

Although the project requires more work to be completed as a small demo I'm confident to be on the right track to have something fun to mess with in the future. I've decided to put an halt to the project because of studies. My goal when i pick up this project again is to create a level in the snow where the alien will need to escape a facility stealthly (or not!) and wreak havoc while doing it!
